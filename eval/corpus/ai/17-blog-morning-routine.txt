# Building a Morning Routine That Actually Sticks

## Introduction

Most people who try to build a morning routine give up within two weeks. They start strong, waking up early and cramming in meditation, journaling, exercise, and a cold shower, only to burn out and go right back to hitting snooze. The problem isn't willpower. The problem is that the routine was never designed to survive a bad night's sleep or a stressful week.

## Why It Matters

A morning routine isn't really about the morning. It's about giving yourself a small, repeatable win before the rest of the day has a chance to throw anything at you. When the first thing you do each day is something you control completely, everything that comes after feels a little more manageable.

## Getting Started

Start smaller than feels necessary. Here's what a sustainable first version looks like:

• Wake up at the same time every day, weekends included
• Drink a glass of water before coffee
• Spend five minutes writing down three things you want to get done
• Get outside for at least ten minutes, even if it's just to the mailbox

That's it. No cold plunge, no ninety-minute gym session, no elaborate journaling template. The goal in week one is consistency, not intensity.

## Overview

Once the basics hold for two or three weeks without a slip, you can layer in more. Add a short walk. Add ten minutes of reading instead of checking your phone. Add a stretch routine. Each addition should feel almost too easy, because the whole point is that you can keep doing it on the days you don't feel like it.

## Key Takeaways

• Consistency beats intensity, especially in the first month
• Anchor the routine to a fixed wake time, not a fixed set of tasks
• Add complexity only after the basics survive a bad week
• A morning routine that requires motivation isn't a routine, it's a hope

## Conclusion

The people who keep a morning routine for years, not weeks, tend to have the most boring version of one. They're not doing anything special. They're just doing the same small thing every day, including the days it would be easier not to. That's the entire trick.
