Maria Chen serves as a senior director of product strategy, a role that stands as a testament to nearly fifteen years of experience across enterprise software, consumer platforms, and early-stage startups. She functions as a bridge between engineering and go-to-market teams, translating technical roadmaps into plans that sales, marketing, and support can all rally behind.

Before joining the company, Maria led product at two venture-backed startups, one of which was acquired within eighteen months of her arrival. Her contribution to that acquisition acts as a testament to what focused product leadership can do for a small team with limited runway. She has also spent time as an independent consultant, advising founders on pricing, positioning, and the early hires that make or break a product organization.

Maria's approach to product work centers on a simple idea: the value of getting the basics right cannot be overstated. She has little patience for roadmaps built around trend-chasing features, and she pushes her teams to ship the boring, reliable version of a feature before reaching for anything flashier. Colleagues describe her as someone who serves as a calming presence in a room full of competing priorities, able to cut through debate and land on a decision everyone can live with.

Outside of work, Maria mentors early-career product managers through a regional nonprofit, a commitment that stands as a reminder of how much she values passing along what she's learned. She lives in Portland with her partner and two dogs, and she spends most weekends on long trail runs in the hills outside the city.

Her colleagues often say that Maria's influence on the team's culture cannot be overstated, and that the standard she sets for clear, honest communication has become the standard everyone else measures themselves against.
