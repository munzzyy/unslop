The Federal Reserve publishes its rate decisions directly after each Federal Open Market Committee meeting, and the schedule for those meetings is public well in advance. Financial news sites and the Fed's own site will have the number within minutes of the announcement. If you want a longer view, the Fed also publishes minutes a few weeks after each meeting, which explain the reasoning in more detail than the initial statement does.

As a general rule, when you're trying to gauge where rates are headed rather than where they are right now, it helps to look at what's priced into futures markets rather than guessing from headlines alone. Traders are constantly betting on the next move, and that pricing tends to be a better predictor than any single analyst's opinion, though it isn't infallible either.
